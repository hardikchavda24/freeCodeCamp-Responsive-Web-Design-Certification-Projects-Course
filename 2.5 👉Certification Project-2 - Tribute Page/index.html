RATAN TATA

Ratan Tata is the former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, one of India's largest and oldest conglomerates. Born on December 28, 1937, in Navsari, India, he is known for his philanthropic efforts and for transforming the Tata Group into a global powerhouse.

Ratan Tata took over as chairman of Tata Group in 1991 and led the company through a period of significant growth and expansion. Under his leadership, Tata acquired several global companies, including Jaguar Land Rover and Corus Steel. He is also known for his commitment to social causes and has been involved in various philanthropic initiatives through the Tata Trusts.

NATIONAL HONOURS

Ratan Tata has received numerous awards, including the Padma Bhushan in 2008 and the Padma Vibhushan in 2014.

BUSINESS HONOURS

  • Economic Times Business Leader of the Year in 2006
  • Forbes Asia Businessman of the Year in 2004
  • Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (KBE) in 2009

OTHERS HONOURS AND REWARD

  • Named among the 100 Most Influential People in the World by TIME magazine in 2010
  • Received the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Economic Times in 2012
